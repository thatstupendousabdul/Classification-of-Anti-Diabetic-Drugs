Classification of Anti-diabetic Drugs

  • Insulin mostly used for the treatment of Diabetes Type 1
  • Oral drugs are used for the treatment of Diabetes Type 2

Classification of Insulin

    Rapid and short acting Insulin:

  • Regular insulin (short acting-50 – 120 minutes)
  • Insulin lispro (rapid acting-peak levels 30-90 minutes)
  • Insulin aspart ( almost same as lispro)
  • Insulin glulisine (almost same as lispro)
  • Inhaled insulin ( rapid acting –peak levels 45-60 minutes)

    • Intermediate acting Insulin:

  • Neutral protamine hagedorn
  • ( zinc + protamine + regular insulin) Only used subcutaneously.

    • Long acting insulin :

  • Insulin glargine : (precipitation, long DOA)
  • Insulin degludec : (very slow release, remain in the solution at physiologic pH)
  • Insulin Combinations: ( 70% NPH+ 30% regular insulin) (HUMULIN 70/30 ) 50%+50% combinations are also avalible.

    • Anti-Diabetic drugs (Oral)

    Sulfonyl Ureas:

      Inhibit K + efflux … thus depolarization…. Ca 2* influx…. Excess release of insulin.
      Reduce synthesis of glucose at hepatic level.
      Increse insulin senstivity.
  • Glyburide
  • Glimepride
  • Glipizide

    • Glinides:

      Reduce postprandial glucose level, should be taken prior to meal.
  • Repaglinide
  • Nateglinide

    • Biguanides:

      (mostly recommended to obese patients)
      Increase cellular uptake,reduce insulin receptor resistane and gluconeogenesis, reduce absorption of glucose from GIT.
  • Metformin (Glucophage)

    • Thiazolidinediones (TZDs):

      (Increase insulin responsive genes thus resistance is reduced.)
  • Pioglitazone (cause bladder cancer)
  • Rosiglitazone ( cause Angina and MI)

    • (α) Glucosidase Inhibitors

      (Inhibit (α) glucosidase enzyme thus prevent breakdown of starch in intestine)
  • Acarbose
  • Miglitol

    • Di-peptidyl peptidase-4 inhibitors:

      Inhibit hormones( incritin and GLP-1 ).
  • Alogliptin
  • Saxagliptin
  • Sitagliptin

    • Sodium – Glucose co-transporter 2 inhibiriors (SGLT-2):

      Inhibit absorption of filtered glucose.
  • Canagliflozin
  • Empagliflozin
  • Ertugliflozin
  • Dapagliflozin
  • Bromocriptin(Increase utilization of glucose and increase release of insulin.)
